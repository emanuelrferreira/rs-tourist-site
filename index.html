FOR HISTORY LOVERS

Discover 3 Must-See Destinations in Busan

Busan’s attractions range from centuries-old Buddhist temples scattered across the mountains and coastline to the city’s pristine beaches with crystal-clear waters. This coastal city offers year-round activities for everyone — families can spend the day at a seaside aquarium, shoppers can explore vibrant districts, and nature lovers can enjoy long hikes with panoramic viewpoints. The temples that line Busan’s shores and mountains feature impressive architecture that delights photographers and history enthusiasts alike.

1. Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

Haedong Yonggungsa Temple is a Buddhist temple located in the northeastern tip of Busan. Built in 1376, it is one of the few seaside temples in Korea — you can enjoy views of the East Sea on one side and beautiful mountains on the other.

Good for:

  • History

2. Beomeo-sa Temple

Beomeo-sa Temple is one of South Korea’s largest and most important sanctuaries. It is located high on the eastern edge of Geumjeong Mountain, far from the busy city center. The temple’s Daeungjeon Hall is a well-preserved example of Joseon Dynasty architecture.

Good for:

  • History

3. Yongdusan Park

Yongdusan Park, located in downtown Busan, is home to some of the city’s most iconic monuments. You can enjoy spectacular views from the top of Busan Tower, which stands 120 meters tall. The park also houses two museums — explore traditional musical instruments at the Museum of World Folk Instruments and discover over 80 Korean model ships at the Exhibition Hall of World Model Boats.

Good for:

  • Couples
  • Families
  • Budget
